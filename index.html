Natural Disasters and Their Effects

A natural disaster is a adverse event caused by Earth's natural processes. Firestorms, floods, hurricanes, tornadoes, volcanic eruptions, earthquakes, tsunamis, storms, and other geologic events are only some of the examples. This visualization will explore their occurrences, effects, and observable patterns under a global scope.

As can be seen, most natural disasters occur in Asia and The Americas. Asia resides in a geologically unstable area that causes natural disasters such as earthquakes and floods. Meanwhile. The United States, which is in the Americas, is densely populated, and hence, it has a heavy infrastructure. All the extreme infrastructure can strain the land and make it vulnerable to geological landslides.

The Effects of Natural Disasters

>

The most significant effects of natural disasters include fatalities and financial damages. However, note that there is no correlation between occurrences of natural disasters and death counts as some countries are more well-prepared to handle such events than others. Take The United States as an example, it has experienced multiple disasters, but the death count is not as high as in China or India. Also, financial damages are not related to the death count, as seen in the two bar charts, where drought caused the most deaths but the least financial damage.
